Benefits of Cardio Workouts

Cardio, or aerobic exercise, is any activity that raises your heart rate and keeps it elevated. It’s one of the best things you can do for your health — improving heart function, burning fat, boosting mood, and increasing longevity.

Regular cardio strengthens your heart, lowers blood pressure, improves cholesterol, and reduces risk of diabetes and heart disease. It also releases endorphins, helping fight stress and anxiety.

Types of Cardio

Steady-state cardio (jogging, cycling) builds endurance. HIIT (high-intensity interval training) burns more fat in less time. Both have benefits — mix them for best results.

Beginners can start with walking, swimming, or jumping rope. Aim for 150 minutes moderate or 75 minutes vigorous cardio per week.

Combine cardio with strength training. See our leg day or full body workout guides for balance.

Cardio isn’t just for weight loss — it’s for living longer and feeling better every day. Start moving today!
